Star Wars

Star Wars was bought by Disney in 2012 for over $4.05 billion. Taking the reins of one of the most successful movie series ever was a risky move by Disney. Many critics believed the company would fail to keep the prestigious figure of Star Wars and turn the legendary series into a giant money grab. Right after buying Star Wars, Disney’s studio chief made it extremely clear that no character or plot point should be seen as a business decision. If this happened, then Disney would be viewed by millions of Star Wars fans as an evil corporation that destroyed the masterpiece they appreciated so much.
Disney has so far been able to avoid being deemed by all as that evil corporation. Many fans of Star Wars actually feel that Disney has revitalized the Series. Right before the pandemic Disney released the first season of the Mandalorian which was extremely successful. The internet fell in love with Baby Yoda and Star Wars fans everywhere were extremely pleased with the show.
The second season for the Mandalorian was released in October of 2020 during the lockdown, causing massive hype around the show. When the lockdown happened in early 2020, many old Star Wars fans who had previously been too busy with work now had free time. The hype of season 2 of the Mandalorian was massive. With memes of Baby Yoda going all over the internet, dormant Star Wars fans everywhere felt nostalgic for the series.
Season 2 of the Mandalorian was able to draw 73% more viewers to Disney Plus when it was released. The show brought millions of fans to Disney’s streaming service, and was able to break a milestone of 60 million subscribers which Disney wasn’t expecting to hit till 2024. The pandemic greatly helped the success of the show, allowing millions of old fans of Star Wars to return to the series they loved so much in the past.
